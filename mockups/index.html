HYRULE CHESS — UI/UX MOCKUPS

Three design directions · click any thumbnail to open the live mockup

Concept A — Codex Corners

Minimal corner badges with active-player halo. No persistent panels over the board. Captures shown as inline chess-glyph chunks next to player name. Move chronicle is a collapsed drawer (bottom sheet on mobile, floating card on desktop). Primary actions live in a small circular dock at the bottom. Active-turn indicator = breathing gold border on the player badge + floating arrow — no banner text.

Concept B — Battle HUD

Full-width top bar with both player slabs + VS chip (like a strategy game lobby). Captures are pill-chunked next to each player's name. Bottom bar is a last-move ticker with action icons flanking it — maximal information density, zero central obstruction. Lobby uses interlocking "ticket" cards with clipped corners. Active turn = entire player slab gets a glowing gold top-border.

Concept C — Tome Rails

Desktop: two 240px side rails bracket the board like an open book. Left rail = Hyrule portrait + captures + menu. Right rail = Ganon portrait + captures + full move chronicle + actions. Turn indicator is a tiny gold pill at the top center. Mobile collapses the rails into a compact top bar (avatars + captures) and a bottom action row — board gets the entire vertical space between. Lobby is a two-column split (hero art left, quest list right).
